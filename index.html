The poem is the consequence of its origin Give me the fruit and I will take from it a see and plant it and watch grow the tree from which it fell. But it is a growing damp and I must go in. Memories fog is rising. Among Emily Dickinsons last words in a letter A woman whom everyone thought of as shut-in, homebound, cloistered, spoke as if she had been out, exploring the earth, her whole life, and it was finally time to go in. And it was.
